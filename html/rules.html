Ground Rules

  • 1. If it has a conveyor belt oven, it’s out (I’m sorry Dominos/Dodo Pizza, but this series isn’t for you).
  • 2. While I would love to ‘One Bite’ this ordeal, our lord and savior Mr. Dave Portnoy already seems to have a monopoly on that (and a pretty shitty one at that, since he takes like 6 bites before giving out a review, these days anyway), I will eat to my fill before documenting this. Therefore, the ‘moreishness’ of the pie is also part of the criteria.
  • 3. I am a firm believer that being a purist is healthy, and would consequently strive, where possible, to start every place’s review with a simple Margherita (will be used interchamngeably with cheese. Don’t you dare come at me) slice, to properly pull out the best qualities of their pizza (i.e., the aforementioned dough, tomatoes and milk2).
  • 4. Disregard rule ‘3’ above. If a place’s Margherita or regular cheese slice is looking underwhelming (and I’d imagine most places’ would), I will opt for a more entertaining slice. However, places that are reviewed on their cheese slice would get bonus points in this regard.
  • 5. Assume every visit that the founder/chief chef has prepared the pizza. Places are entitled to follow up reviews on different pizzas, but joints with abysmal reviews get no second chances.
  • 6. To preserve the quality of the pie, pizzas would be picked up from the store, and the first slice would be tried straight out of the oven. In extenuating circumstances, where delivery is required, slight leeway will be given for any assumed drop in quality.
  • 7. Re-eatability of the slice (this is a house of leftovers) would positively factor in to the review (In english, a sub par slice might become a good slice if its just as good/better on reheat. However, a good slice does not become a bad slice should it be deemed inedible after 12 hours in the fridge).
  • 8. Unique (to this side of the world) slices such as the New York Style, Neapolitan, Deep Dish, Apizza, just to name a few, would attract bonus points.
  • 9. Have fun. None of these rules really matter in the face of good food.
  • 10. Don’t we all just love those nice round numbers?
