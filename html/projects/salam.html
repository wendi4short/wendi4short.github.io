Salam

"Let’s set the scene: EXTERIOR. DISHEVELLED BUILDING – DAY. Thes smell of roasted chicken permeates my nostrils. Three to 5 uniformed employees stand around an evidently unheated food display cabinet, staring aimlessly at what could very well have been their first customer of the day. The time is past noon. Ambient noise is ever present, but almost seems to fade out, just as it approaches the barrier between restaurant and road. It is just us here, taking in the majesty of what we hope to be the source of a succulent Italian meal, and the elements seem to respect that. But there is no barrier – not really. We are outdoors, in an open-plan shopping complex, standing beside a hot, summer, sidewalk (iykyk). "

ENTER, MR. SALAM himself, sun beaten skin, a permanent scowl and the look of someone that just finished smoking a cigarette, or maybe is just about to inhale one, idk (it’s a weird look to characterise. Sue me). All the uniformed employees are hushed into silence. As though terrified that the big man’s mere acknowledgment of their present would have them swallowed whole, or perhaps – even worse still – sliced and diced like the mound of skewered meat not inches away from their hushed breath… "

"Needless to say, the vibes are absolutely phinimino. "

"Salam is a shawarma/pizza spot that’s been around for a whiiiile, but seems to have escaped everybody’s radar. Now I am uncertain as to whether that is due to their lack of quality (which is not the case), or their lack of reach (which also doesn’t seem to be the case, considering their location). Either way, it is probably nestled firmly within that ‘the desired clientele already patronise the establishment’ space, because I cant imagine you stay in business in lagos for 15+ years without consistency in all its forms. But it also just looks old. Not bad old. Not really. Moreso the old of ‘I have been here long before you knew me, and I will be here long after you forget’. The squat building’s white tiled floors and glass doors (yes, yes, that’s the oh so esoteric marker I am going for to delinieate ‘then’ from ‘now’ – tiled floors and glass. Lmao. But I stand by it. If you see ‘built a while back’, it just looks different, notwithstanding any renovations or similar designs of today) harken back to an age where quality was respected, and décor a personality trait. Not the formulaic build-a-house assembly line structures we have erected all over the place today. "

"Now this is a joint who’s owners were alongside the mega plaza gang in the preparation and procurement of their shawarma shaped goods – “We are the only ones that still make shawarma like this. Except maybe Mega Plaza. Imported everything. Real.”, I can still hear him saying. A high pedestal! has evidently been set with the Mega plaza boys, for comparisons to them being considered the be-all-and-end-all of what is required to scream: QUALITY GOODS HERE! I don’t fault the man. Perhaps living 5 years or so in the past, but hey. This isn’t a shawarma podcast (or a podcast at all, for that matter), so what do I know? Oh, and did I mention they were Lebanese? I think this should help put this and the previous paragraph into better context, lol. "

"Back to business however, they did go on to say that the same standards apply to their pizza however, namely, all imported ingredients, dubious reference to Mega Plaza (ok, I’m exaggerating now. They didn’t compare their pizza to MP (although, now that I think about it, I should probably stop by there sometime, and give them the ol’ review dazzle)). Do I agree? I mean, of course I do. I doubt such a trustworthy gentleman would lie to me, and I have dealt with many an untrustworthy gentleman in my lifetime. "

"So my expectations were… well, not HIGH, but still. Pretty up there. It looked like the kind of establishment that had a stone oven hidden in there somewhere that had just SEEN things, you know? Well, I don’t ‘know’, or anything, because I didn’t actually enter that day, since all the good stuff was happening afuera (they have done a big remodel though, so all of this is pretty obsolete now, but oh well). Then it arrived! Oh how I joyed and splendored at the sight of it. In a nondescript white branded box was my next conquest, and oh boy was I thrilled. "

"Ehhhhhh, pretty good. I mean, no complaints really, It sort of just felt like a slightly elevated version of 005’s (see immediately preceding review) slice. The benefit here is that you rarely have a bad thing to say about such a generic pie, but the immediately prevalent downside is the fact that you tend to not have very much good to say either. Cheesy, tomatoey, doughy goodness, plain and simple. I mean, I’m not one to fix what isn’t broken, but I am also not one to declare the unbroken piece of glass as the next Il Duce. Nonetheless, I think slices like this (especially considering their inexpensive nature) should be cherished. Not every painting is a Picasso. And What you get is consistently palatable food, at a decent price. "

"Now I’m no bloody communist or anything, but to me, that’s a pretty good deal. It’s a pizza that I would, if I’m hungry and in the area, would definitely stop by to pick up. Your humble Narrator is therefore inclined to give this one a +961 out of Beirut on a cooooool Friday sunset, especially for their willingness to play ball for so long. But don’t expect a warm welcome, you’re only there for one thing only, and that’s good enough. "
