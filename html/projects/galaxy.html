Galaxy

"So this was my first recommendation. Well, not recommendation from me, but recommendation from the outside. Does that make sense? I think that makes sense. Stop trying to make it seem like that doesn’t make sense. It does. The expectation for this series was that these spots were going to be found in the wild, while swinging from trees and squinting through the thick blanket of vehicle exhaust that inevitably covers every expressway in the state. "

"However, I cheated. Someone told someone who told someone about this place, which led to me being offered a slice. As the paragon of ‘zzategrity that I am, I vehemently refused. If I was conducting any review, I would make my way to the store with my own hands, pick up the box with my own hands, and make the required fiscal exchange with my own hands. "

"However, my worldview doesn’t always match my reality… view. "

"The pizza was good. I wasn’t particularly impressed by their offering, as it seemed to be a fairly ‘pop pizza’ kinda establishment, but levelled up. So I played into their strengths, and went for the cheesy meaty offerings. And they were good! But in an upgraded Dominos way. The dough was better, and the oven was better, so the crust was better, and the toppings were better. In fact, I believe they have a wood oven. I am not entirely sure. But it was still, at its core ‘American fast-food pizza’ kind of pizza. Perhaps I’ll give it the crown of being the best of the American fast-food pizzas. It’s not a high bar to cross, but still. A crown is a crown, after all. "

"This is also where I reveal that it was my first delivery order (there really isn’t much to be desired internally either, so it just wasn’t worth the trip. Honest). While this is a pretty big rule to break, I believe it is worth a mere footnote in this instance because, again, Galaxy is more dominos than artisan, so, the doughy crust has some staying power. In cases such as this, I like to use my well known slogan: “If the cheese is still cheesy (IYKYK), then the dough is good to go” (patent pending). Therefore, I believe it pretty safe to say that the quality was affected even one bit. "

"So whats the verdict then? Ehhhhh? Its tough to say. While it is marketed to be a higher tier pizza, and I’m sure their ingredients are also of a higher ilk, I do put it squarely in the fast food pizza territory, I am sorry to say. Albeit, at the top of this group. So this leaves us with a bit of a dilemma. Unlike its ‘competitors’ (in my book, anyway), it doesn’t consider itself one of the bottom feeders, so they don’t offer the same incentives the others do, such as deals, discounts, BOGOFs, etc. These other institutions are under no illusions about their appeal, and work to ensure that quantity over quality is front and centre of their marketing, since they naturally cant compete with quality. "

"Galaxy however, is at a weird mid-point, where they charge semi-premium prices (high end of bottom feeders), yet offer no incentive to walk into the door. Do I blame them? Obviously not. The moment they do that tgey become one of the masses. But for me, they don’t quite tick the necessary… boxes (hehe (the joke is pizza, btw)) to make me a repeat offender. "

"I think it’s time to bring this milquetoast yapfest to an end, as ive said pretty much all there is to say about these fine Galactic gentlemen. In light of all this, I believe I’ll give them a solid “will eat if free, but not if its yikkers, however, will consider over others, should the circumstances present themselves, which they probably wont, because they wont be set up in the same category”, or something like that, idk. "
